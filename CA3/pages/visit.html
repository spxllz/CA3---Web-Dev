Where every moment is a postcard-worthy adventure

Boat Trips

Embark on exhilarating boat trips to the Bass Rock, Fidra (some say the inspiration for Robert Louis Stevenson's Treasure Island), and other nearby islands. Experience the thrill of seeing the islands upclose with stories and tales from local experts at the Seabird Centre. There is also lots of sea-life to be seen along the way such as seals and the occasional pod of dolphins.

The Law

North Berwick Law is a prominent volcanic hill that stands overlooking the town, offering visitors a glimpse into its history with its Napoleonic era signal station nestled just below its summit. This iconic landmark once boasted an arch at its peak crafted from a whale's jawbone, which unfortunately collapsed in June 2005. With its rich historical significance and breathtaking views, North Berwick Law provides an enriching experience for those eager to explore the natural and cultural wonders of the area.

Beaches

Among North Berwick's primary draws, its beaches stand out, boasting stretches of golden sands and rock formations shaped by the relentless forces of the sea. The East Sands, in particular, offer a retreat with its tide-filled boating pond and paddling pool. These picturesque shores are perfect for a sunny summer's day, offering opportunities for relaxation, seaside strolls, and water-based activities. North Berwick's beaches are a must see for any visitor rain or shine with access available for all from the beach wheelchair hut at the harbour.

Golf

North Berwick features not one, but two prestigious golf courses within its charming confines. Golfers can tee off amidst the scenic beauty of the West Links and the Glen (also known as East Links), each offering unique challenges and breathtaking views. Golf lovers can explore several other renowned courses dotted along the coast, providing a diverse range of golfing experiences to suit all skill levels.

John Muir Way

Explore the pathways and trails that trace the footsteps of John Muir as he journeyed across East Lothian, each marked with distinctive John Muir signposts. These trails lead you through both coastal and countryside landscapes, unveiling the charming scenic corners of the town along the way.

Tantallon Castle

Tantallon Castle stands as a testament to Scotland's medieval history, its imposing ruins evoking tales of battles and sieges from centuries past. The castle dates back to the 14th century and is a must see being only 3 miles east of the town. Despite its mostly ruined state, Tantallon Castle offers visitors a glimpse into its storied past and the strategic significance it once held along Scotland's eastern coast.
